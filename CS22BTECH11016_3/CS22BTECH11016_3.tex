\textbf{12.13.1.14}:
Given that the two numbers appearing on throwing two dice are different. Find the probability of the event the sum of numbers on the dice is $4$.\\
\vspace{\baselineskip}
\textbf{Solution}:\\ 
When a dice is thrown, the number of observations in the sample space is $6 \times 6 = 36$. Let $A$ be the event that the sum of the numbers on the dice is 4 and $B$ be the event that the two numbers appearing on throwing the two dice are different.

Therefore, we have:

\[ A = \left\{
\begin{matrix}
(1,3) & (2,2) & (3,1)
\end{matrix}
\right\}\]

\[ B = \left\{ 
\begin{matrix}
(1,2) & (1,3) & (1,4) & (1,5) & (1,6) \\
(2,1) & (2,3) & (2,4) & (2,5) & (2,6) \\
(3,1) & (3,2) & (3,4) & (3,5) & (3,6) \\
(4,1) & (4,2) & (4,3) & (4,5) & (4,6) \\
(5,1) & (5,2) & (5,3) & (5,4) & (5,6) \\
(6,1) & (6,2) & (6,3) & (6,4) & (6,5) \\
\end{matrix}
\right\}\]

\[ (A.B) = \left\{
\begin{matrix} 
(1,3) & (3,1)
\end{matrix}
\right\}\]
Therefore, we can calculate the probabilities:
\begin{align}
P(B) &= \frac{30}{36} = \frac{5}{6} \\
P(A.B) &= \frac{2}{36} = \frac{1}{18}
\end{align}
Let $P(A|B)$ represent the probability that the sum of the numbers on the dice is $4$, given that the two numbers appearing on throwing the two dice are different.

Therefore, we have:
\begin{align}
P(A|B) &= \frac{P(A.B)}{P(B)} \\
&= \frac{6}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{18} \\
&= \frac{1}{15}
\end{align}
Therefore, the required probability is $\frac{1}{15}$.
